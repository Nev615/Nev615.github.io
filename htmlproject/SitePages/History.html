Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egypt, spanning from 3100 BCE to 332 BCE, is renowned for its monumental achievements, including the Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, and advances in mathematics, medicine, and writing (hieroglyphics).

Greco-Roman Period

Beginning in 332 BCE with Alexander the Great's conquest, Egypt became a center of Greek and Roman culture. The city of Alexandria emerged as a hub of learning and trade.

Islamic Period

After the Arab conquest in 641 CE, Egypt became a leading Islamic cultural and economic center. Cairo, established in 969 CE, remains a symbol of this era.

Ottoman and Modern Period

Under Ottoman rule from 1517 to the 19th century, Egypt served as a strategic province. Modernization began during Muhammad Ali's rule, paving the way for independence in the 20th century.

Contemporary History

Modern Egypt was shaped by the 1952 revolution, leading to the establishment of the Republic. Key events include the construction of the Aswan High Dam and Egypt's pivotal role in Middle Eastern geopolitics.
